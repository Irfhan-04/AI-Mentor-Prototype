Many students at large commuter universities miss lectures because they work part-time jobs during the day, and existing note-sharing groups on Discord and GroupMe are disorganized, inconsistent in quality, and often disappear when the semester ends. We are building NoteSync, a lecture-note marketplace scoped to a single university's large intro courses (200+ students), where note-takers upload notes after each class and earn a small per-download credit, while students who missed class can browse notes by course and date instead of searching scattered chat threads. Our first target is our own campus: three intro courses with a combined 900 enrolled students, where we have already secured verbal agreement from two teaching assistants to recommend the platform to students who request lecture recordings. We will monetize through a small subscription fee per course per semester rather than per-note payments, since per-note pricing discourages consistent uploading. Our biggest technical dependency is verifying note quality without manual review at scale, which we plan to address early by capping each course to five vetted note-takers chosen by TA recommendation rather than allowing open uploads. We expect to reach 150 paying students in our pilot course within the first semester based on current group membership size.
