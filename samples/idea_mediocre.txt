College students often struggle to find compatible study partners for difficult courses, especially in large lecture halls where they don't know their classmates. We are building StudyBuddy, an app that matches students in the same course based on shared class schedule, preferred study times, and self-reported learning style, connecting them through in-app chat so they can form study groups more easily than posting in a class GroupMe. The app will use a simple algorithm to suggest matches and will notify users when a compatible partner joins their course section. We believe this addresses a widespread problem because nearly every student has experienced isolation while studying for a hard class. Our plan is to launch a free version at our university and expand to other campuses once we have traction, eventually introducing premium features like AI-generated study guides. We have not yet spoken with students to confirm they would use a new app instead of existing GroupMe or Discord groups they are already in, but we believe the matching algorithm will provide enough additional value on its own. Monetization will likely come from advertising or a freemium model, though we have not decided which yet. We expect strong organic growth through word of mouth once students see how useful the matching feature is in practice.
